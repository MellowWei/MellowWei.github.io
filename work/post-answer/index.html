Post Answer

A native iOS tool for inspecting an AI answer after it has already been generated.

Post Answer treats the response as a document the learner can inspect, seal, test, and reinterpret. The visual language borrows from editorial proofing: numbered sentences, evidence brackets, answer seals, verdict stamps, a drawable signature, and a tear-off learning slip. No dashboard score is created.

02 / Starting point

Where Post Answer begins

Context

Generative AI can frame a question, select evidence, connect sources, and propose a conclusion. A student may receive a fluent, useful answer without seeing which parts of that work they performed, delegated, or skipped. Most AI learning modes intervene before or during generation. Post Answer starts once the answer is already there.

Challenge

The product needed to create useful friction without becoming another authority. A weakness in the answer could not automatically become a weakness in the learner. Fluency, hesitation, grammar, and brevity also had to remain ambiguous signals, especially for ESL learners and people with different response styles. Every interpretation needed a way to be contested.

My role

I originated the product thesis, developed the learning-sciences and epistemic-agency framing, audited the competitive boundary, iterated the interaction model from Answer Audit to a closed-answer learning encounter, designed the bilingual interface, and built the interactive prototype and evaluation protocol.

Design question

How might a post-answer interaction help a learner encounter one previously invisible gap—without allowing the model to decide whether they understand?

Conceptual signal

A fluent answer can conceal delegated epistemic work.

The product focuses on the operations hidden by the finished output: framing, selecting, connecting, interpreting, and revising.

Competitive boundary

The differentiation starts after generation.

ChatGPT Study Mode, Gemini Guided Learning, and LearnLM already scaffold learning. Post Answer accepts an answer from any platform, then makes it an object of audit or a prompt for independent action.

Design risk

A second AI can become a second authority.

Early Answer Model and Gap Map concepts still positioned the system as judge. The V1 pivot gives the learner the final verdict over every surfaced gap.

Equity constraint

Friction is not proof of non-understanding.

Hesitation, brevity, grammar, or borrowed wording may reflect language, pressure, access needs, or a poor probe. Every interpretation must remain contestable.

01

Theory review

Use epistemic infrastructure, knowledge building, metacognitive demand, and learner-standing research to define which parts of judgment a post-answer product should preserve.

02

Competitive boundary audit

Compare current AI learning modes by when they intervene, what object they evaluate, who owns the verdict, and whether the output can travel across platforms.

03

Prototype three models—Answer Audit, dual-model Gap Map, and closed-answer encounter—to expose how interface structure can quietly return or remove authority.

04

Run a 50-response discovery survey, then 15 interviews combining a recent de-identified AI answer, think-aloud probes, learner contestation, and a 24-hour transfer follow-up.

Working hypotheses

What the current evidence allows me to test—not claim.

H1

Confidence may arrive before retrieval.

A learner can feel informed after reading a coherent answer without having reconstructed the load-bearing mechanism independently.

H2

Action may surface a gap better than explanation.

Closing the answer and attempting retrieval, boundary variation, or transfer may make missing connections more encounterable than another AI-generated analysis.

H3

Contestability may reduce false positives.

Allowing learners to mark expression friction, invalid probes, or uncertainty may prevent the system from converting every struggle into a deficit claim.

H4

Value must justify friction.

Students are unlikely to spend 10–15 minutes after every answer unless the surfaced gap feels real, previously unnoticed, and consequential to their own goal.

04 / Interaction model

The answer and the learner need separate records.

The interaction uses two objects. Audit examines the generated text and produces a contestable Answer Model. Learn looks at one action performed without the source and produces a learner-owned gap. The model can generate probes and suggest interpretations, but it cannot issue the final verdict on understanding.

01

Two objects

Do not collapse a weakness in the answer into a weakness in the learner.

02

Judgment first

Record the learner’s initial confidence before showing system reconstruction.

03

Action before diagnosis

Use retrieval, boundary, and transfer probes to create an observable attempt.

04

Learner verdict

Accept, rename, contest, reject, or leave a candidate gap unresolved.

05

Minimal trace

Keep the output local, deletable, and oriented toward the next action—not surveillance.

05 / Interface details

Four moments where the learner keeps the final say

The iOS flow is built around four concrete moments: inspecting the answer, working without its wording, interpreting the difference, and deciding what to keep. The large boards below show the interaction states in detail, while the prototype above keeps the full sequence playable.

01 / Audit mode

Tap the sentence. Change the lens.

Audit is no longer a dashboard of analysis cards. It is a numbered reading surface. Claim, Mechanism, Assumption, and Evidence lenses highlight different parts of the same text while a margin note states exactly what the system is inferring.

  • Sentence-level rather than card-level inspection
  • External evidence demands remain unresolved
  • Answer object kept separate from learner object
  • Every reconstruction remains contestable
02 / Learn mode

Seal the wording. Keep the learner’s action visible.

The answer folds into a sealed source object after confidence is pinned. Retrieval, Boundary, and Transfer probes produce an attempt that belongs to the learner. Reopening is allowed and recorded without penalty.

  • Initial judgment pinned before the probe
  • Three mechanism-focused probe types
  • Reopening remains a learner choice
  • Attempt kept without automated scoring
03 / Contestability

Turn the difference into a signed interpretation.

Before and after are connected on an interpretation desk, not converted into a performance score. The learner names the friction, then Accepts, Renames, Contests, or Rejects the candidate difference and signs that choice.

  • Before / after comparison without diagnosis
  • False-positive alternatives remain visible
  • Four explicit verdict actions
  • Final interpretation receives a learner signature
04 / Learning trace

Issue a learning slip—not a learner score.

The output takes the form of a tear-off slip containing the learner’s interpretation, process trace, judgment signature, and one delayed-transfer prompt. It remains local, revisable, and visibly deletable.

  • One signed interpretation, not a dashboard
  • Local, revisable, and deletable
  • 24-hour delayed-transfer prompt
  • No default reporting to an institution

06 / Current state

What exists today

Post Answer now has two legible product modes, sentence-level answer inspection, closed-answer probes, learner-controlled verdicts, a drawable judgment signature, a local Learning Trace, and a delayed-transfer return. The embedded native iOS demo uses a deterministic sample flow so reviewers can reproduce the complete interaction without submitting data or waiting for an AI response.

Evidence boundaryNo 50-person survey or 15-person interview study has been completed. Those numbers are the next-cycle recruitment targets. The project does not yet claim validated learning gains, reliable diagnosis of understanding, long-term transfer, or objective truth verification.

What changed in my thinking

The project became stronger when the system stopped trying to name the learner’s gap.

The project changed when I stopped asking how AI could reveal the learner’s hidden gap and asked what interaction would let the learner encounter, inspect, and name a gap themselves. That shift moved the Answer Model behind the learner’s action and turned contestability from a policy statement into a concrete interaction requirement.

What I learned
  1. Separate built capability, design hypothesis, and validated effect on every product surface.
  2. Treat hesitation and fluency as ambiguous signals rather than measures of understanding.
  3. Design AI behavior around who retains the right to interpret—not only whether an output is accurate.
Next iteration
  1. Recruit the planned 50-response survey and 15 think-aloud concept-test participants.
  2. Test probe validity and accessibility across ESL, slow-response, and varied communication preferences.
  3. Evaluate live probe quality, repeated use, and 24-hour transfer without overstating causality.

01 / Overview

Frame the system before drawing the screen.

Context

At NetEase Youdao, I worked on the companion experience for translation hardware used across international travel, business communication, and study-abroad situations. The app had to connect physical earbuds with live interpretation, two-way conversation, recording, device controls, and saved translation records.

Challenge

The difficulty was not adding another translation function. Every task depended on several invisible layers at once: hardware connection, left and right earbud state, microphone permission, audio routing, language direction, speaker turn, network quality, and AI generation. When those layers were collapsed into similar-looking screens, users had to infer what the product was doing before they could communicate.

My role

As a UX design intern, I synthesized the existing product requirements and international-use references, mapped the mode and state architecture, explored interaction directions, refined onboarding and mode switching, and contributed more than forty standardized UI components plus over fifteen illustration guidelines. The public case focuses on my selected interaction and system contributions rather than reproducing the internal working canvas.

Design question

How might a translation-earbud companion make device, audio, and AI state understandable at a glance—while letting each real-world conversation mode feel purpose-built?

02 / Design thinking

Evidence → insight → design decision.

I treated the Figma workspace as evidence of a product ecosystem, not as a gallery of screens. The analysis connected internal product requirements, competitor patterns, international-use contexts, visual references, and a detailed state inventory. No fabricated respondent counts are presented: the portfolio distinguishes reference synthesis and design exploration from formal user research.

Signal 01

The handoff was invisible

A user could know that translation was available without knowing whether audio was entering the phone, leaving through the earbuds, or waiting on a connection.

Signal 02

Similar cards hid different behaviors

Simultaneous interpretation, two-way earbud conversation, and face-to-face phone interaction looked related but demanded different turn-taking and attention.

Signal 03

Live AI needed a visible present tense

Listening, translating, paused, disconnected, and saved states had to be perceivable without reading explanatory copy during a conversation.

Design synthesis

What the product needed to make visible.

01

Device state must precede feature state

The interface should establish which hardware is connected, where audio will play, and whether both earbuds are available before asking users to choose a translation task.

02

Each live mode needs a distinct stage

Simultaneous interpretation is transcript-led; face-to-face translation is turn-led; recording is time-led. Reusing one generic layout would hide the behavior users need to learn.

03

Original language is part of trust

Translation should not replace its source. A visible original, language direction, and current-sentence state let users inspect what the system interpreted.

04

Recovery belongs inside the moment

Permission, connection, network, and generation problems should preserve the current task and offer a direct recovery action instead of ejecting users into settings.

03 / Strategy

One system grammar; five different interaction stages.

The design direction combined a familiar iOS interaction grammar with a restrained ‘soft technology’ language. Consistency lives in navigation, status, cards, typography, and motion; differentiation lives in the spatial model of each task.

01

Show readiness first

Connection, battery, audio route, and language direction appear before a user commits to a live mode.

02

Design by attention

Transcript-led, turn-led, and time-led tasks use different composition instead of inheriting one template.

03

Keep the live present visible

Waveform, active speaker, current sentence, and pause state communicate what is happening now.

04

Preserve inspectability

Original text, translated text, recording, and saved history remain available after AI mediation.

01 / Device home

Establish readiness before exposing capability

The connected-device home combines hardware identity, left/right/case battery, readiness, settings, and situational mode entry. The experience remains in context while connecting or recovering instead of sending the user to a separate technical surface.

  • Left, right, and case battery remain glanceable
  • Modes are framed by situation and audio behavior
  • Connecting and offline states preserve the same mental model
02 / Simultaneous interpretation

A transcript-led stage for continuous listening

Simultaneous interpretation prioritizes source language, translation direction, speaker, current sentence, waveform, and audio route. The user can pause the session or reveal the source without losing the translated stream.

  • Current listening and translating state remain explicit
  • Original and translated text occupy separate layers
  • Earbud output is confirmed without explanatory interruption
03 / Face-to-face and two-way

Make turn-taking part of the interface architecture

Face-to-face mode treats the phone as a shared object with two directional speaker zones. Two-way earbud mode treats audio as private and uses the phone as a visual reference. The shared design system does not erase this behavioral difference.

  • Speaker zones clarify who should talk next
  • Language direction stays visible between turns
  • Saved records preserve source and translated meaning
04 / Selected contribution · Y03

A compact scan-and-lookup flow inside the same product language

The scan translation exploration separates multi-line scanning from single-line lookup, gives the camera state a clear selection boundary, and lets users inspect a word without leaving the source. It shows how the system scaled beyond live audio while retaining familiar status and control patterns.

  • Multi-line and single-line modes are visibly distinct
  • Selected words remain anchored to their source
  • Definition details appear without destroying scanning context
05 / Component system

Systematize consistency without cloning every screen

Shared tokens govern mode icons, connection and AI states, cards, typography, and illustration rhythm. Each feature then composes those parts according to its own attention model—device-led, transcript-led, turn-led, time-led, or camera-led.

  • 40+ standardized UI components
  • 15+ illustration guidelines
  • Mode accents remain accessible against calm shared surfaces

05 / Outcome

A clearer translation system across device, audio, and screen.

40+standardized UI components
15+illustration guidelines
30%+reported key-task time reduction

The work consolidated the experience around a visible device-to-mode-to-live-state model. Standard components and illustration guidance created a shared grammar, while differentiated interaction stages made simultaneous interpretation, conversation, recording, and scan tasks easier to recognize. The internship documentation reports a reduction in key-task completion time after the onboarding and mode-switching restructure.

Evidence boundaryThe contribution counts and task-time improvement are reproduced from the internship résumé/project documentation. The available public material does not include the study sample, baseline, protocol, or statistical analysis, so the portfolio presents the 30%+ figure as a reported project result rather than an independently verified causal claim.

Reflection

Real-time translation is not one AI response. It is a chain of handoffs that the interface must keep understandable.

This internship taught me to design beyond the visible screen. Hardware readiness, audio behavior, permissions, language direction, turn-taking, model latency, and saved records all shape whether a person can trust and direct a live AI experience. The strongest system did not make every mode look identical; it made the shared rules learnable and the behavioral differences unmistakable.

What I learned
  1. Map hidden hardware and system dependencies before simplifying the visible flow.
  2. Use mode-specific spatial models for continuous listening, shared conversation, recording, and camera interaction.
  3. Keep original input and current AI state inspectable when translation mediates consequential communication.
Next iteration
  1. Run comprehension testing across first-time device setup and rapid mode switching.
  2. Validate recovery for offline, permission, low-battery, and interrupted-session scenarios.
  3. Explore stronger terminology review and source-trace controls for business and academic use.

01 / Profile

Critical inquiry,
made tangible.

Trained in Philosophy and Communication at the University of Southern California and now completing an M.S.Ed. in Learning Sciences & Technologies at the University of Pennsylvania, I bring questions of meaning, evidence, and power into product design.

Across NetEase Youdao, Ctrip, and Lanhu, I have designed AI-assisted creation tools, educational and translation hardware experiences, and high-stakes travel journeys. My research focuses on interpretive agency and contestability: when AI systems teach, recommend, or evaluate, people should be able to inspect how they were interpreted, challenge the output, and make the final decision their own.

03 / Practice

Research is not a phase before design.

It is the evidence layer that keeps a product's interpretation of people contestable.

“Judgment is a design material.”— Mellow Wei
01

Ground

Start with lived situations, not an abstract user. Locate the decision, its consequences, and whose interpretation carries weight.

02

Interpret

Turn patterns into explicit design claims. Separate what the evidence supports from what the team is still assuming.

03

Return

Design the interface so people can inspect, challenge, and act—not merely accept a system's recommendation.
